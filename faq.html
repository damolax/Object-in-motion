How the sculptures move, how they are made, what they require and how an original commission begins.

01

What is a rolling ball sculpture?

A genre of kinetic art in which marbles travel through a carefully engineered path of ramps, loops, spirals and mechanisms. Every Objects In Motion sculpture is uniquely designed around movement, rhythm and craftsmanship.
02

How long does a sculpture take to build?

Creation time depends on scale and complexity. Most works take approximately four to twelve weeks from design to completion; larger custom projects can require more time.
03

Are the sculptures handmade?

Yes. Every work is handcrafted by Robert and no two pieces are exactly alike.
04

What materials are used?

The sculptures are created primarily from stainless steel, often with glass marbles. Wood, lighting and found or repurposed objects are sometimes incorporated.
05

Do the sculptures require maintenance?

Maintenance is minimal. Basic cleaning of the track and marbles with rubbing alcohol or water helps keep the sculpture running smoothly for many years.
06

Can the work be shipped internationally?

Yes. Sculptures can be carefully packed and shipped to many destinations worldwide, with appropriate documentation prepared for the shipment.
07

Can a sculpture be designed for a particular space?

Yes. Photographs and measurements of the intended location can guide the scale, orientation, viewing angles, lighting and overall character of a commission.
08

How do I begin a commission?

Start with a no-obligation conversation. Share your space, approximate size, ideas and timing. Robert can then develop a concept, scope and proposed next step.
09

When is price confirmed for a commission?

The current commission process confirms price, deposit and timing after Robert has collected the project information and prepared a conceptual direction.
10

What happens before a completed work is shipped?

The sculpture is thoroughly tested, and detailed photographs and video can be sent for final approval before payment and shipment.
11

Can a rolling ball sculpture be wall-mounted?

+

A work can be discussed as wall-mounted, freestanding or tabletop depending on its scale, structure and intended location. Photographs and measurements help determine the appropriate direction.

12

Do rolling ball sculptures make sound?

+

The moving marbles and mechanisms naturally create sound. The character and level of sound depend on the sculpture, materials, speed and room, so this should be considered when planning a home, office or public setting.

13

Do motorized sculptures need power?

+

Motorized works require a suitable power connection. Voltage, adapter, cable route, speed control, lighting and activation features are confirmed for the specific sculpture or commission.

14

Can a sculpture be designed for an office, hotel or public space?

+

Yes. A professional project can consider visitor flow, viewing distance, scale, sound, power, durability, installation, maintenance and the experience the organization wants to create.

15

How is the price of a commission decided?

+

Price is confirmed after the intended scale, complexity, materials, mechanisms, timeline, delivery and installation needs are understood and a concept direction has been discussed.

16

Can Robert reproduce a sculpture that has already sold?

+

Every sculpture is an original. A past work can inspire discussion about scale, movement, materials or character, but a new commission would be developed as a distinct work rather than an exact copy.
